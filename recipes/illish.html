Illish Mach

Ingredients

  • 4 pieces of fresh Ilish (Hilsa) fish
  • 1 large onion, finely sliced
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 4-5 tablespoons mustard oil
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, crushed
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 cup water
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Marinate 500g of Hilsa fish with ¾ tsp salt and ¾ tsp turmeric powder, and set aside.
  2. Cut 100g of brinjal into 5-cm chunks and slit 5 pcs of green chillies. Before you begin, also make a paste of 18g salt, 6g turmeric, and 50g water.
  3. Heat 40g mustard oil in a pan till it starts to smoke lightly and changes color to a pale yellow.
  4. Add ¼ tsp kaalo jeere (nigella seeds) to the oil and let it splutter for a few seconds.
  5. Add the marinated fish pieces to the pan and fry them for about 2 minutes on each side until they turn golden brown. Remove the fish from the pan and set aside.
  6. In the same pan, add the brinjal chunks and green chillies. Fry them for about a minute or until they turn slightly brown.
  7. Add the paste of salt, turmeric, and water to the pan. Stir well and cook for about a minute or until the water evaporates.
  8. Add 350ml hot water to the pan and bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 5 minutes.
  9. Add the fried fish pieces to the pan. Cover it with a lid and let it cook on low heat for about 10 minutes or until the fish is cooked through.
  10. Turn off the heat and let it rest for a few minutes before serving hot with steamed rice.
